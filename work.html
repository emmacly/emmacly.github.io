Practicum | Grades 1–9 Physical Education

Overview

During my first practicum, I had the opportunity to teach Grade 1–9 physical education, gaining experience in programming and planning lessons for a wide range of grade levels. I focused on assessing student performance, providing feedback, and collaborating with my cooperating teacher to create inclusive and engaging physical education experiences.

Key Contributions

  • Created and adapted lesson and unit plans for diverse grade levels
  • Implemented classroom management strategies to support learning
  • Assessed student skill development, participation, teamwork, and understanding of game rules
  • Provided constructive feedback to help students improve
  • Collaborated with cooperating teacher to reflect and improve lessons

Impact & Growth

This practicum helped me develop skills in lesson planning, classroom management, and differentiated instruction. It reinforced my ability to create engaging, inclusive physical education experiences and adapt teaching strategies to meet diverse learner needs.

Student Teacher Volunteer| Grades 10–12

Overview

Volunteering in the ACCESS and ALP classes, I provide individualized support to students with moderate to severe learning and adaptive behaviour needs, assisting in functional academics, social communication, independence, and vocational development.

Key Contributions

  • Delivered personalized instruction and classroom support
  • Tailored activities to student abilities and interests
  • Implemented accommodations including assistive technology and structured supports
  • Fostered an inclusive and supportive classroom environment
  • Built meaningful connections with students to support learning and growth

This experience strengthened my skills in differentiating instruction, fostering inclusion, and adapting to a wide range of learning needs, while deepening my ability to connect with students meaningfully.
